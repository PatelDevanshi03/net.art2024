Beyond Boundaries: The Digital Architecture of Human Connection

Curated by Devanshi Patel

The online exhibition "Beyond Boundaries: The Digital Architecture of Human Connection" delves into the creative spirit of net art, showcasing works that challenge traditional notions of space, communication, and the relationship between humans and technology.

This exhibition examines how, in the digital era, net art creates new relationships. Robert Adrian's 1982 feature "THE WORLD IN 24 HOURS" breaks down geographical borders by connecting audiences worldwide through live communication. In the project "MOBILE IMAGE" (1975 - Ongoing), artists Kit Galloway and Sherrie Rabinowitz invited collaborators to create performances designed for the screen via the use of real-time video conferencing. Jon Rafman's "NINE EYES OF GOOGLE STREET VIEW" (2008 - Ongoing) blurs the boundaries between virtual and away-from-keyboard life by using digital pictures to link viewers to actual geographical areas. Tsunamii.net's "ALPHA 3.4" (2002) looks to the depths of the ocean to explore the blurred lines between cyberspace and physical locations.

Though these works employ different approaches, they all show how net art has the power to forge new connections and alter our understanding of the world. They create a powerful portrait of the changing terrain of connection in the digital age, where real and virtual environments merge and technology serves as a means to promote fresh forms of communication and understanding between people.

1. NINE EYES OF GOOGLE STREET VIEW (2008 - Ongoing) by Jon Rafman

~ Enables viewers to establish relationships with places that have been documented through digital photos.

2. MOBILE IMAGE (1975 - Ongoing) by Kit Galloway, Sherrie Rabinowitz, and Collaborators

~ An ongoing project that utilizes satellite technology to connect participants across vast distances in real-time, fostering a sense of global community.

3. THE WORLD IN 24 HOURS (1982) by Robert Adrian

~ A groundbreaking live satellite broadcast showcasing events happening around the world in real-time, offering a glimpse into a future of global interconnectedness.

4. ALPHA 3.4 (2002) by Tsunamii.net

~ An immersive online art encounter that involves audiences in a strange and dynamic virtual world. The artwork encourages investigation of the complexity of digital presence and the limitless possibilities of cyberspace through its compelling images and interactive features.
