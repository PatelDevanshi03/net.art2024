What is stress?

Stress is the body's and mind's reaction to things or circumstances that endanger our health. It is the body's response to any form of pressure or expectation.
Numerous factors, including job, relationships, money troubles, or traumatic experiences, can cause stress. Stress physiologically causes chemicals
like cortisol to be released, raising blood pressure and heart rate. Long-term stress can cause anxiety, depression, and heart problems, among other health problems.
Concentration problems, memory problems, and unfavorable thought patterns are examples of its effects. Excessive or sustained stress is harmful, whereas moderate stress might improve performance.

But NO WORRIES!! Its effects can be minimized and general well-being can be increased by coping mechanisms like exercise, mindfulness, and social support.
